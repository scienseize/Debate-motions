The product itself is toxic

Skin whitening products are toxic and have been proven to be toxic. These whitening products work by hindering the formation of melanin in the skin, however, they can also cause a number of health issues in your body. Using toxic skin whitening products can lead to symptoms such as rashes, discoloration, scarring, inflammatory issues, damage to the nervous, digestive, and immune system. Furthermore, constant exposure to the dangerous chemicals found in these products could lead to the destruction of melanin in a person's skin. Melanin is a pigment responsible for the color of our skin and when it is reduced, the skin will have less protection against UV rays–with this, something as simple as going out in the sun could potentially lead to conditions such as melanoma.

Moreover, a handful of skin whitening products contain mercury which is bad for the skin. Mercury is a chemical element that can be dangerously corrosive when it makes contact with a person’s skin. Mercury has been proven to damage the brain, kidneys and the nervous system as a whole–mercury getting into the skin can also cause rashes, blotchy spots, and give the skin a grayish tint. A study conducted by the 2022 Annual Report of the American Association of Poison Control Centers' National Poison Data System documented 1143 single exposures to mercury or compounds containing mercury. In conclusion, 30 individuals suffered moderate toxicity and 5 had severe toxicity. How much more if we continued to allow the implementation of skin whitening products that actually do contain mercury? These products have been left onto the shelves of stores all across the world for anyone to purchase, oblivious to the harms these products can cause. Other common skin lightening agents – such as glutathione, hydroquinone, retinoids, and steroids – inhibit the production of melanin in a person’s skin. All of the mentioned skin lightening agents have been known to have negative effects on people who use it. Moreover, products that contain hydroquinone have been found to be associated with squamous cell carcinoma, the second most common form of skin cancer, through case reports.

The regular exposure to these products are just not worth the dangers they may pose, not when these dangers could potentially be a cause for something as deadly as cancer. With the sheer amount of symptoms stated and studies conducted in regards to the toxicity these products pose to the skin, skin whitening must be banned
