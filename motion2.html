Vaping: an act of inhaling and exhaling nicotine or other chemicals as vapor using a handheld e-cigarette device.

Arguments

Generally leads to multiple health risks

Although vaping has less health risks than smoking, e-cigarettes are not risk-free. Most e-cigarette use e-juice that contain nicotine, which is an addictive and harmful substance when mixed with other toxic chemicals from e-juices. In long-term exposure, nicotine raises blood pressure, narrows the arteries and causes addiction. Vaping can also cause breathing problems. The particles the user inhales while vaping causes inflammation and irritation in the lungs. This can lead to lung damage like scarring and narrowing of the tubes that brings air in and out of the lungs. Vaping e-liquids exposes the user to several potentially harmful chemicals, including diacetyl, a flavoring compound known to cause bronchiolitis obliterans or popcorn lungs. Vaping leaves the user likely vulnerable to getting asthma and other lung conditions such as lipoid pneumonia.. EVALI(e-cigarette or vaping product use-associated lung injury), originally known as VAPI(Vaping-associated pulmonary injury) are mainly effects of vaping or the use of other e-cigarette devices. It is a new kind of e-cigarette associated disease that causes severe lung conditions that require hospitalization. Among cases documented, 96% had undergone hospitalization including some who died. Nicotine, diacetyl and other harmful substances are mainly found in e-liquids or commonly known as e-juices. Additionally, when the e-liquid heats up, more toxic chemicals are formed. Short-term effects of vaping include coughing, dry and irritated mouth and throat, shortness of breath, etc.

Environmental Impact

E-cigarettes come in different variations, including disposable e-cigarettes which are mostly made with non-biodegradable components such as plastic, metal and batteries. Most vapes and other e-cigarette devices are made from plastics. Plastic waste especially is a rising environmental concern in every country. It takes hundreds of years for plastics to fully degrade and only a small percentage can only be recycled. Some plastics that came from e-cigarettes cannot be recycled due to containing toxic chemicals. It can also be consumed by animals resulting in injuries or death. E-cigarettes are powered by batteries, specifically ion-lithium batteries. If damaged or placed under extreme heat environments, it may cause explosions and fire. E-liquids contain a mixture of nicotine and other chemicals that are considered a hazardous waste. Hazardous waste is generally harmful to both humans and the environment if not disposed of correctly. There’s a possibility that it may contaminate our waters which would affect the population and it can be poisonous to fishes and other aquatic faunas. E-cigarettes release aerosols into the air which can contribute to air pollution, ozone layer depletion and greenhouse gasses. The e-cigarette industry is responsible for a sizable carbon footprint from manufacturing, packaging, and shipping products around the globe. The emissions from this supply chain can contribute to climate change.
