Social and Economic Disparities

According to the ASEAN Trade Union Council, the Philippines has the third highest economic and social inequality rate in Southeast Asia. This problem is not limited to personal wealth. The growing disparity between the Philippines' richest and poorest citizens also affects land distribution, educational and vocational opportunities, and basic welfare programs. Economic inequality has been pronounced and even more so for the last decade,The indigenous people are frequently the ones that suffer the most. Roughly 1.7 million members of the 100 ethnic groups that make up the Philippines continue to face the greatest disadvantages, with discrimination and a lack of opportunities posing significant obstacles. State universities should prioritize students from lower socioeconomic backgrounds, providing them access to quality education and bridging the gap between the rich and the poor. This would promote social mobility of improvement within the Philippine's economic background. Prioritizing a merit-based admission system within state universities will be able to prioritize students from lower social standing who produce quality achievement within their academic standing, proving the hard work they put out for their education, should be recognized and be uplifted by the country’s system. This would erase the supposed disparities and inequality driven by discrimination, building a foundation for those struggling with their economic status, as the youth should be able to excel within their educational rights without worrying about their limited resources.

Terms and Definitions

Economic Disparities: refer to the unequal distribution of income, wealth, and opportunity among different groups in society.

Arguments

Freedom of education

The freedom of education is a human right stated in the constitution. The constitution states that education in general will be protected and every citizen has the right to quality education at any level which includes the college level and to take appropriate steps to make it accessible. While state universities were made with the idea of letting students who cannot pay the tuition in mind, that doesn't mean that a group of students should be excluded. No one should be excluded for something that is outside their control. A student should not sacrifice their education just because they’re rich, a school like UP has a good education and because of the lack of tuition giving students more academic freedom.

People should not be excluded from getting an education

People have the free will to choose what college they enter, there isn’t a set rule that if you’re rich then you shouldn’t go to state universities. It is not fair for the students to refuse a spot in a top university which is a good school because of something they cannot control. Public universities were made to make education accessible and as stated in the constitution, people have the right to a quality education and if the students see themselves getting quality education at a state university then we should be able to respect their choice because they are ultimately doing it to better themselves.

Just because a person is rich doesn’t mean they didn’t work hard.

Students who are in a higher income bracket have more advantages compared to those who went to less funded public schools. But that doesn’t mean they didn’t work. If they can afford it they go to review centers where they get reviewers, take mock exams and get tips on how to enter a state university. But that doesn’t mean that will give them a spot in a state university, it's only a stepping stone and the student is still expected to do the work. Even with the resources it's all useless unless you actually put in the work into studying and reviewing.
